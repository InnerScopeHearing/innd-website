          Market opportunity

          A massive, underserved market, with a regulatory tailwind.

          Tens of millions of adults

          Adult hearing loss in the United States alone affects tens of millions of people, and only a minority use hearing aids today, held back by cost, prescription requirements, clinic visits, and stigma.

          The OTC regulatory shift

          The FDA's creation of an over-the-counter hearing-aid category opened a non-prescription path through pharmacies, mass-market retailers, and direct e-commerce. That shift is the single largest structural change to the hearing-aid market in a generation.

          Where InnerScope sits

          At the intersection of OTC regulation, established retail relationships now operated through OTCHealth, and a digital-health and teleaudiology technology base inherited from the iHEAR Medical acquisition. Family-clinic operating instinct still shapes which products get built.

          Where we're headed

          The next chapter: putting affordable hearing care where people already are.

          This section describes goals and initiatives and contains forward-looking statements. See important disclaimers in the footer.

          Pharmacy as the next hearing counter

          There are more than 90,000 pharmacy locations across the United States, staffed by trusted pharmacists, yet very few have a licensed hearing professional on site. The over-the-counter category opens a path to bring simple, affordable hearing care to the same counter where people already pick up their prescriptions. It is the white space we are most focused on.

          Growing the direct-to-consumer engine

          OTCHealthMart.com, operated by OTCHealth Inc., is the consumer storefront for the iHEAR and HearingAssist brands. The goal is to widen the catalog for the 55-plus shopper, from hearing aids and personal sound amplifiers into the broader everyday self-care basket, and to deepen the relationship with a customer base built over years.

          Technology and teleaudiology

          The iHEAR acquisition brought an app-based, remote-fitting and at-home screening foundation. We intend to keep building on that base so better hearing can be tested, fitted, and supported without a clinic visit, which is exactly what the OTC shift makes possible.

          A transparent shareholder community

          After years in the public markets, we believe the most valuable thing we can offer shareholders is consistency: regular updates as developments become public, real perks tied to our storefront, and an open line. Excitement earned through execution and clear communication, not promises.
